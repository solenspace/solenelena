## 2. Product: Hannlys

**What it is:** A curated marketplace for digital products that are personalized to each buyer in real time. Buyers don't download generic files — they tell Hannlys what they need (voice-first), and the product is built for them on the spot.

**What it is NOT:** Hannlys is not another Etsy, Gumroad, or template store. It is not a file-hosting platform. It is not a place to sell pre-made assets and hope they fit. Every product on Hannlys is a living blueprint that becomes something personal.

**The core innovation:** Creators don't upload finished products. They upload **Seed Products** — blueprints paired with agent instructions. When a buyer purchases, the Hannlys agent asks a few questions (voice-first), then generates a fully personalized version of the product, fitted to that buyer's specific needs.

**Tagline:** *"Made for you, not for everyone."*

### Name origin:

Hannlys comes from "handy" — the idea of something useful, ready, always there when you need it. The word was turned into a name, becoming its own identity. Hannlys doesn't need a backstory rooted in tragedy or mythology — it's rooted in utility. The name *is* the promise: whatever you need, it's handy.

## 3. Core Concepts

### Seed Products

The fundamental unit of Hannlys. A Seed Product is not a file — it's a set of instructions that produce a personalized product when activated by a buyer's input.

**Every Seed Product contains four required parts:**

1. **Base template / blueprint** — The skeleton: a structured layout, schema, plan grid, or document framework. The "shape" the personalization fills in. Can be JSON-based configuration, a Notion structure, a PDF layout, a spreadsheet schema, or any structured format.

2. **Tailor instructions (the agent prompt)** — What the agent should ask the buyer, how it should reason about their answers, what variables it fills, what tone the output uses. This is the creator's authored intelligence — their expertise encoded as agent guidance.

3. **Example outputs / samples** — 2–3 fully personalized examples the creator pre-generated. Displayed on the product page so buyers can see what they'll receive before committing. These are the storefront.

4. **Brand assets** — Cover image, accent color, typography choices. Each Seed Product gets its own micro-brand inside Hannlys, reflecting the creator's identity and the product's personality.

### The Personalization Moment (Voice-First)

The buying experience on Hannlys mirrors Elena's UX:

1. Buyer finds a Seed Product (browsing, search, recommendation)
2. On the product page, they see examples + description
3. They record a short voice note describing what they need — or answer the agent's questions by voice
4. The Hannlys agent (powered by Elena's engine, persona TBD) processes the voice input, reasons through the creator's Tailor instructions, and generates a fully personalized version
5. The buyer receives their product — either as a downloadable file/bundle or as a live interactive artifact hosted on Hannlys (creator chooses the format per product)

This voice-first approach is the core differentiator. No other marketplace asks the buyer *what they need* before delivering. Every other platform assumes the generic product is good enough.

### Re-Personalization

Buyers get lifetime access to re-run the personalization. Life changes — a new job, a new fitness goal, a new team size — and the product adapts. "I changed jobs — redo my plan." This transforms a one-time purchase into an evolving asset.

### Seed Versioning

Creators can update their Seed (improve prompts, refine templates, add variables). Old buyers keep access to the version they purchased. Optionally, they can upgrade to the new version and re-personalize. Like software releases for digital products.

### Reviews & Ratings

Buyers rate the personalized output — not the generic product page. This means ratings reflect actual delivered value, not marketing promises. High-rated Seeds surface in discovery; low-rated ones sink.

## 4. The AI Agent

**Engine:** Elena — Solen's agentic loop. The same streaming architecture, LLM orchestration, and tool-execution pipeline that powers Elena's enterprise workflows.

**Persona on Hannlys:** The agent adopts a Hannlys-specific persona (name TBD). The archetype is **The Tailor / Artisan** — patient, precise, quietly proud. Asks the right questions, builds something fitted to the buyer. Not flashy, not chatty — focused on craft.

**Voice characteristics:**
- "Let me make this yours."
- Asks 2–3 focused questions, not 10
- Confirms with short, specific phrases: "Got it. Building your 12-week plan now."
- Never over-explains the AI — just delivers the result
- Warm but expert — not robotic, not bubbly

**The agent is NOT the headline.** The product being personalized is the headline. The agent is the means, not the story. Buyers should remember the product they got, not the AI that made it.

## 5. Target Audience

### Buyers — Primary: Ambitious Consumer / Prosumer

- Self-improvement-driven individuals: creators, freelancers, students, fitness enthusiasts, entrepreneurs, career changers
- People who buy digital products today on Gumroad/Etsy/Notion but settle for "close enough"

### Creators — Supply Side

- People who already sell digital products (Notion templates, workout plans, business kits, study guides)
- Experts who have knowledge worth packaging but don't want to build a SaaS
- The pitch to creators: "You bring the expertise. Hannlys brings the personalization engine and the audience."
- Creators must apply — Hannlys is curated, not open. Every Seed Product is reviewed before listing.
- Creators' output quality is reflected in personalized reviews, not just product-page marketing. This rewards substance over salesmanship.

## 7. Positioning & Differentiation

### The problem Hannlys solves:

Digital products today are generic. A "productivity template" assumes everyone has the same job, schedule, and goals. A "workout plan" assumes everyone has the same body, equipment, and fitness level. Buyers download a file, spend hours customizing it to fit their life, and still end up with something that's only 70% right. The product that actually fits them was never on the shelf — because it can't exist until someone asks what they need.

### How Hannlys is different:

| vs. Etsy | Etsy sells static files in an ocean of noise. Hannlys sells personalized outputs from curated blueprints. Discovery is intentional, not accidental. |
|---|---|
| vs. Gumroad | Gumroad is a checkout page — creators bring their own traffic, buyers get a file. Hannlys is a destination marketplace with voice-first personalization built into the buy moment. |
| vs. Notion Marketplace | Notion templates are locked to one platform and one format. Hannlys is format-agnostic — PDFs, web apps, spreadsheets, interactive tools. And every output is personalized, not generic. |
| vs. Whop | Whop is communities and memberships. Hannlys is products — discrete, personalized, deliverable. Different unit of value entirely. |
| vs. Hiring a freelancer | A freelancer takes days and costs hundreds. Hannlys delivers a personalized product in 30 seconds for $5–$50. Same quality intent, 100x faster. |

### Core differentiator:

Not the number of products — the **promise**: whatever you buy on Hannlys was made for you, not for everyone. The combination of voice-first input, curated Seeds, and real-time personalization makes Hannlys the only marketplace where the product doesn't exist until the buyer shows up.

### The one takeaway for prospects:

**"Made for you, not for everyone."**

It's not about AI. It's about the absurdity of settling for generic products when a personalized one takes 30 seconds to create.

She pays $12. Downloads her plan. It fits perfectly.

Three months later, she's stronger. She comes back: *"I bought a pull-up bar. Update my plan."* Re-personalization. New program. Same $12.

## 12. Security & Trust Positioning

**Voice data:**
- Voice input is processed for personalization only — never stored long-term, never used for training
- Transcription happens in real time; audio is discarded after processing

**Buyer data:**
- Personalization inputs (goals, preferences, context) are tied to the buyer's account
- Data is used to generate the product and enable re-personalization — not shared with other buyers or creators
- Creators see anonymized aggregate data (e.g., "60% of buyers selected beginner level") — never individual responses

**Creator IP:**
- Tailor instructions (the agent prompt) are never exposed to buyers — they see only the output
- Seed blueprints are protected; buyers receive the generated output, not the source template

**Infrastructure:**
- Same Supabase PostgreSQL, OAuth, and encryption infrastructure as Elena
- Built on Solen's production-grade backend (Rust/Axum, SQLx, JWT auth)

## 14. Voice & Copy Guidelines

### Do:

- Lead with the outcome, not the tech ("Your 8-week plan, built in 12 seconds" > "AI-personalized fitness template")
- Use concrete buyer scenarios — name the person, the need, the result
- Write warmly — Hannlys is inviting, not intimidating ("Tell us what you need" > "Input your parameters")
- Show the before/after: generic template → personalized product
- Use "yours" and "you" constantly — this is personal
- Emphasize the voice interaction naturally — "just tell Hannlys" / "say what you need"

### Don't:

- Use "AI-generated," "AI-powered," "powered by AI," or any AI-hype framing — the AI is invisible
- Over-explain how the personalization works — buyers care about the result, not the pipeline
- Use passive voice ("products are personalized" → "Hannlys personalizes your product")
- Promise infinite customization — the creator defines the boundaries; personalization is guided, not freeform
- Compare directly to Etsy/Gumroad in marketing copy — let the experience speak; don't name competitors

### Tone calibration:

- **Headlines:** Warm, declarative, short. ("Made for you." / "Tell us. We'll build it." / "Your product. Not theirs.")
- **Body copy:** Clear, encouraging, specific. One benefit per sentence. Never more than 3 sentences per block.
- **CTAs:** Inviting and action-oriented. ("Browse products" / "Start personalizing" / "Apply to sell" / "Tell Hannlys what you need")
- **Creator-facing copy:** Empowering, respectful. Treat creators as experts and craftspeople, not vendors.
- **Product pages:** Example-led. Let personalized samples do the selling. Minimal description, maximum show-don't-tell.

### Banned terms:

- "AI-generated" / "AI-powered" / "powered by AI" — the AI is the means, never the headline
- Any term that makes Hannlys sound like a generic marketplace (when used to describe Hannlys itself in marketing copy)
- Hype language: revolutionary, game-changing, cutting-edge, next-gen, disruptive

### Preferred language:

- "Personalized" / "tailored" / "fitted" / "built for you" — craft language
- "Seed Product" (internal/creator-facing); "product" (buyer-facing) — buyers don't need to know the Seed abstraction
- "Creator" (acceptable for Hannlys — unlike Elena, Hannlys honors the creator identity)
- "Voice" / "tell us" / "say what you need" — natural voice-first language
